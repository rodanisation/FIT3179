Exploring Car Accidents Data in Victoria (2015 - 2018)



This web page aims to examine car crash data in Victoria in 2015 - 2018 inclusive, by using a number of graphs and charts to identify high risk areas, explore behvaiour & common accident types and finally, look into fatality rate.



First a look into displaying each accident as a dot based on its reported locations

Accidents in Victoria

In this map, the aim is to place each accident on the map based on its exact reported coordinates to examine high risk areas. It is evident that Metropolitan Melbourne shows a dense red colour indicitaing a higher number of reported accidents that correlates to a higher population density.

Next, highlighting accidents trend for each year

Line Trend By Year

After investigating accidents in each area, a look into trends for each year can clearly demonstrate an overall decline in the total number of accidents. These findings can support the Victorian Government actions that aims to reduce accidents on the road.

Note that, the cut-off at the end of the lines is due to the summation of accidents for each month being calculated at the beggining of each month.

Then, investigate human behaviour at the time of accident

Pie Charts to Exlore Human Behaviour

To investigate human behaviour such as drunk drivers and hit & run accidents, the findings demonstrate a positive outlook and similar results. Drunk drivers and hit & run accidents comprise a miniscule percentage of the total reported accidents. Hence, showing high rate of responsibility and less chaotic behaviour.
